## Constitution of the USDX Module
**The Stablecoin**
**Network: Solana | Version: 1.0 (Independent)**

### Preamble
The USDX Module is an initiative to create an asset-backed stablecoin. It is designed as a self-contained financial system that does not depend on the liquidity or success of other modules. Its activation is conditioned solely on the accumulation of sufficient reserves and the auditing of its mechanisms.

### Article 1: Nature and Conditions of USDX
USDX will be an asset-backed stablecoin. Its issuance is **constitutionally prohibited** until the `USDX Stablecoin Reserve` (**`F9k4xRUrNvb6qrhY2c72ytNuqokVQZUh1VXLNi5XzsAz`**) demonstrates the required minimum collateral and the corresponding external audit is completed.

### Article 2: Independence of USDX
USDX operates as an independent module. Its value and stability do not depend on LXR or XLS.

- **Threshold:** Any action requiring multi-sig authorization must be approved by at least **4 out of 6 members**.
- **Scope:** The multi-sig council is responsible for authorizing high-risk operations, including but not limited to: emergency pauses, liquidity migration, protocol upgrades, and any movement of funds from vaults that are not governed by automated smart contracts.

### Article B: Transparency and Community Notification
1.  **Public Accountability:** The community owns 90% of the project's resources. Therefore, all movements of funds from any vault except the personal wallets of the founder team (which are necessary for operational privacy) **MUST** be publicly announced in advance on the official social media channels and community platforms (Discord, Telegram, Twitter).
2.  **Notification Requirements:**
    - **Timing:** At least 48 hours before the transaction, unless it is an emergency security measure (which must be explained post-facto within 24 hours).
    - **Content:** The announcement must include the reason for the transfer, the amount, the source and destination addresses, and the multi-sig signatures obtained.
3.  **90% Community Ownership:** This principle underpins the obligation to inform. Any decision affecting the collective funds (the 90% not allocated to founder team personal wallets) must be transparent and, where applicable, subject to community governance votes.

### Article C: Security Mechanisms (Shared Infrastructure)
Although modules are sovereign, they share common security infrastructure for robustness:

1.  **Audit & Security Fund:** Tokens allocated for code security are locked in **`8jxRTukX3DBgF5dM4VHwamAhwAf3PodhkCBd8XhsKJQu`**.
2.  **Burn Address:** The account **`GdGWLfdzSexEBV6j9pNZhAmsqXvMkW9uJ76iWMtPLXqu`** is designated as a burn address for any module that may implement token burning in the future.
3.  **Oracle Hierarchy:** Modules requiring external price feeds (LXR, XLS) will implement a 4-layer oracle system: Pyth (primary), Chainlink (fallback), Appraisal Oracle (human certification), and Circuit Breaker (discrepancy >8% triggers a 24-hour soft freeze).
4.  **External Audits:** Prior to mainnet launch, the smart contract suite will undergo comprehensive audits by firms such as OtterSec, Zellic, and Kudelski Security.

### Article D: Activation and Amendments
1.  **LXR Module:** Active from Genesis Block.
2.  **XLS Module:** Remains locked until conditions in its constitution are met. Its activation requires a qualified DAO vote and multi-sig ratification.
3.  **USDX Module:** Remains locked until its reserve conditions are met and audited.
4.  **Amendments:** Modifications to fundamental articles (supplies, exchange mechanisms, constitutional locks, income distribution, oracle mechanisms) require:
    - A qualified DAO vote of the affected module (quorum 20%, approval 60%).
    - Multi-sig ratification (3/6).
    - Public announcement and 30-day notice period before implementation.
